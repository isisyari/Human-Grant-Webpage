Relevant Reads

Research papers, books, peer-reviewed journal recommended by the Krebs Center research team

God, Human, Animal, Machine- by Meghan O'Gieblyn

A strikingly original exploration of what it might mean to be authentically human in the age of artificial intelligence.

Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI- by Ethan Mollick

Wharton professor Ethan Mollick explores how AI is reshaping work, learning, and creativity—urging us to partner with smart machines as co-workers, coaches, and collaborators. With real-world examples, he shows how to harness AIs power while staying grounded in our humanity.

AI Engineering: Building Applications with Foundation Models- by Chip Huyen

A practical guide to building AI apps with foundation models—no deep AI expertise needed. It covers tools, techniques, and workflows for fast, reliable AI development.

Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks from the Stone Age to AI- by Yuval Noah Harari

Nexus explores how information networks have shaped human history—from ancient myths to modern AI—and how they now threaten our future. It is a sweeping look at truth, power, and the urgent choices we face in the age of artificial intelligence.

Research Projects

Ongoing and past projects supported by the Human Grant on the field of AI and the humanities.

Chicago History Museum (CHM)

The HUMAN project & Chicago History Museum are developing ChiBot, a generative AI tool that uses CHM’s collections to map and represent Chicago’s diverse communities. ChiBot will extract spatial data, analyze bias, and help fill representation gaps using materials like oral histories & the Encyclopedia of Chicago.

Sweet Water Foundation

The HUMAN project and Sweet Water Foundation explore ethical AI in urban ecology through a course, internships, and a capstone exhibition connecting AI with community, design, and sustainability.

Ragdale

Ragdale and Lake Forest College present the HUMAN Residency, a multi-year program for artists exploring AI, the humanities, and social justice. It includes a group residency (2025), solo residency (2026), and a final showcase (2027), connecting artists with students through creative collaboration and public events.
